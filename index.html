2.1 Literature Survey

Existing Systems & Background

An analysis of current agricultural machinery maintenance approaches and their shortcomings.

Existing Systems in Agriculture Maintenance

Existing agricultural machinery maintenance systems largely rely on time-based or manual inspection schedules. Solutions such as FMIS (Farm Management Information Systems) provide basic record-keeping but lack intelligent wear detection capabilities. Industrial IoT platforms used in large-scale farming are cost-prohibitive and inaccessible to smallholder farmers in developing regions.

Studies on predictive maintenance in agriculture (Paraforos et al., 2017; Kayode et al., 2020) highlight that reactive maintenance causes up to 40% more downtime than condition-based approaches. However, these studies rely on sensor-heavy hardware setups that are not mobile-friendly.

Key Problems Identified

⚠️
Manual Inspection

Farmers rely on visual inspection alone, leading to missed early-stage wear, unexpected breakdowns, and costly emergency repairs.

🚫
No Traceability

Spare parts lack verifiable provenance. Counterfeit parts flood rural markets, causing mechanical failures and safety hazards.

❓
Poor Compatibility Knowledge

Farmers and local mechanics lack access to compatibility databases, leading to wrong part selection, voided warranties, and repeated failures.

2.2 Research Gap

Identified Research Gaps

Critical gaps in the current body of research that this project directly addresses.

01

No Real-Time Mobile Wear Detection

No existing system provides real-time, mobile-based AI detection of mechanical wear specifically for agricultural machinery components such as tyres and V-belts.

02

No Trusted Spare Part Tracking

There is no blockchain-based traceability solution designed specifically for agricultural spare parts in developing markets, leaving farmers vulnerable to counterfeit components.

03

No Intelligent Compatibility Recommendations

Existing recommendation systems do not account for agricultural machine families, functional equivalence, and multi-factor soft filtering (price, material, brand).

04

Lack of Visualization Tools for Farmers

No accessible 3D visualization tool exists that maps detected spare parts to interactive machine models, making diagnosis unintuitive for non-technical farmers.

2.3 Research Problem

The Core Problem

❝

Farmers face unexpected machine failures, counterfeit spare parts, and significant difficulty identifying correct replacements due to a critical lack of intelligent, accessible, and integrated digital tools — resulting in prolonged downtime, financial losses, and reduced agricultural productivity.

💸

Financial Loss

Unplanned downtime and wrong part purchases cost smallholder farmers significantly during peak harvest seasons.

⏱️

Productivity Loss

Machine downtime during critical farming periods leads directly to crop losses and missed market windows.

🔩

Counterfeit Parts

Fake spare parts cause accelerated mechanical failures, unsafe operating conditions, and voided warranties.

2.4 Research Objectives

Project Objectives

Main Objective

Build an intelligent, integrated system for spare part lifecycle management in agricultural machinery — combining AI wear detection, blockchain traceability, compatibility recommendation, and 3D visualization into a single mobile-accessible platform.

01

Detect Wear Using AI

Develop a mobile-compatible computer vision module using YOLOv8 and MobileNetV2 to detect early-stage wear on tractor tyres and V-belts.

02

Ensure Authenticity via Blockchain

Implement a blockchain-based lifecycle ledger with QR-code identity, smart contracts, and trust scoring for spare part verification.

03

Recommend Compatible Parts

Build a multi-stage Random Forest recommendation engine that applies hard and soft filters to suggest compatible spare parts with confidence scores.

04

Visualize Parts in 3D

Create an interactive 3D visualization layer powered by ResNet-50 recognition, mapping detected parts to interactive machine models.

Module 3.1

Computer Vision Wear Detection

Real-time, mobile-friendly AI detection of mechanical wear on agricultural machinery components.

🚨 Problem

Farmers cannot detect early-stage mechanical wear without specialist knowledge. By the time damage is visible to the naked eye, it is often too late — resulting in sudden breakdowns during critical farming operations.

📦 Dataset

  • Tractor tyre wear images (labelled: good / worn / critical)
  • V-belt degradation images (labelled: intact / cracked / broken)
  • Augmented with rotation, noise, and brightness variations

🧠 Models

MobileNetV2 — Classification YOLOv8 — Object Detection

Module 3.2

Blockchain Lifecycle System

A decentralized trust layer ensuring every spare part has a verified, immutable digital identity.

🚨 Problem

Counterfeit spare parts are rampant in rural agricultural markets. Without verifiable provenance, farmers unknowingly install fake components that fail prematurely and create safety hazards.

✨ Features

  • Unique QR code issued per spare part at manufacture
  • Immutable blockchain ledger records every ownership transfer
  • Full lifecycle history accessible via QR scan
  • Trust score computed from provenance data
  • Off-chain storage for large files (warranty docs, images)

Smart Contract Logic

1
Part manufactured

Digital identity minted on blockchain with QR code

↓
2
Ownership transfer

Smart contract records distributor → retailer → farmer chain

↓
3
Warranty validation

Contract auto-validates warranty based on transfer timestamps

↓
4
Maintenance logged

Service events appended to immutable ledger

↓
5
Trust score generated

Algorithm computes authenticity score from full history

✅ QR-Based Identity ✅ Immutable Ledger ✅ Smart Contracts ✅ Off-Chain Storage ✅ Trust Score System
